F.A.Q.

General Questions

Where does the curriculum for Girls Who Code meetings come from?

As part of the national organization Girls Who Code, all of the curriculum that we use comes from lessons designed by the organization. Girls can choose a variety of projects of different skill levels, so that they can pursue something that interests them.

How long do clubs run for?

Clubs are run on a semesterly basis. For the Fall 2018 semester, our clubs will start near the beginning of September and run until early December. While meeting durations will vary slightly based on location, typically meetings are 1 to 1 1/2 hours long.

For K-12 Students in the St. Louis Area

Who is eligible to join Girls Who Code - WashU?

We're looking for middle- and high-school girls who live in the Greater St. Louis area to participate in our program. For more information visit our Join as a Participant page.

Does it cost money to join Girls Who Code?

Nope! All curriculum is provided for free from the national organization Girls Who Code, and any materials and programs will be provided by our group for no cost during meetings.

Can I attend any of the locations?

Our WashU locations are open to anyone from the St. Louis area and surrounding communities. Satellite locations at local schools are only open for students attending that school.

The meetings this semester don't fit in my schedule, is there a way for me
to still be involved?

No worries! If the current time doesn't work for you this semester, email us to let us know that you're interested and check back in a few months. We're looking into hosting more groups next semester to give students more options to join.
